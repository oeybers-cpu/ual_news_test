Welcome Authors

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the UAL Newsletter. Please review our focus areas, use the submission form below, and feel free to contact our editorial team with any questions.

Focus Areas

We welcome articles that explore the dynamic landscape of academic literacies, particularly within the African context. Our focus areas include:

Writing Center & Language Support:

  • Innovative practices in writing centers and language units.
  • Supporting multilingual writers and speakers.
  • Tutor training and peer mentoring programs.

Teaching & Learning:

  • Effective pedagogies for academic literacies development.
  • Integrating academic literacies across disciplines.
  • Student engagement and learning strategies.

Language Unit:

  • Sharing editing services for academic texts.
  • Providing transcription services for research data.
  • Developing language resources and workshops for students and staff.
  • Conducting research on academic language practices and support.

Conferences & Community Engagement:

  • Insights from academic literacies conferences.
  • Community-based learning and outreach initiatives.
  • Collaborations between institutions and communities.

Multilingualism & Technology:

  • Role of digital tools in supporting diverse learners.
  • Addressing challenges and opportunities in multilingual contexts.

Submit Your Article

To submit your article, please complete the Google Form below. You will be asked to provide your article file and some basic information.

Instructions:

  1. Click the "Submit" button within the form.
  2. Upload your article file (Word or RTF format).
  3. Fill in the required fields (Title, Abstract, Keywords, Author Bio, etc.).
  4. Submit the form.

Our editorial team will review your submission and contact you regarding the next steps.

Note: For multimedia files (images, audio, video), please upload them to a shared Google Drive folder or email them separately to the editorial team. Include a link to the files in the Google Form if using Drive.
