Guidelines, Process, and Contact Information

Welcome Authors

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the UAL Newsletter. Please review the submission guidelines, use the provided form for uploading your article, and feel free to contact our editorial team with any questions.

Submission Guidelines

Article Length: Articles should be between 800 and 1,100 words.

Content:

  • Articles should be original and unpublished.
  • Focus on topics relevant to African languages, literacies, education, and cultural studies.
  • Ensure clarity, coherence, and a well-structured argument or narrative.
  • Use accessible language suitable for a broad academic audience.

Style and Formatting:

  • Use a clear, readable font (e.g., Times New Roman, size 12).
  • Double-space the text.
  • Include page numbers.
  • Provide a concise title (max. 15 words) and a brief abstract (50-100 words).
  • Include 3-5 keywords.

Citations:

  • Follow the Harvard citation style for in-text references and the reference list.
  • Ensure all sources are accurately cited to avoid plagiarism.
  • Reference list should be alphabetized and placed at the end of the document.

Example (Harvard): Ngũgĩ, W.T., 1986. Decolonising the mind: the politics of language in African literature. London: James Currey.

Author Bio: Include a short author biography (50-75 words) with your affiliation and contact details.

Submission: Articles should be submitted as a Microsoft Word (.docx) or Rich Text Format (.rtf) file.

Upload Your Article

To submit your article, please complete the Google Form below. You will be asked to provide your article file and some basic information.

Instructions:

  1. Click the "Submit" button within the form.
  2. Upload your article file (Word or RTF format).
  3. Fill in the required fields (Title, Abstract, Keywords, Author Bio, etc.).
  4. Submit the form.

Our editorial team will review your submission and contact you regarding the next steps.

Note: For multimedia files (images, audio, video), please upload them to a shared Google Drive folder or email them separately to the editorial team. Include a link to the files in the Google Form if using Drive.
